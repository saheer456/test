About Carevia Foundation

Carevia Foundation began during the COVID-19 crisis with a small group helping struggling families. Over time, it grew into a registered nonprofit working to empower women, support education, and improve healthcare in underserved communities.

The story of Carevia Foundation began in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic. A small group of friends came together with one simple intention — to stand beside those who were struggling in the most difficult of times. What started as small acts of kindness and support during a global crisis soon grew into a shared commitment to create lasting change. Over the course of six years, that spirit of compassion evolved into something greater: Carevia Foundation. Today, we are a registered nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering women, nurturing education, and expanding access to healthcare in underserved communities.

  • Our Journey

    COVID Response – Providing immediate relief to families in need during the pandemic. From Friends to Foundation – Turning volunteer-driven social work into a structured platform for long-term impact. Now and Beyond – Expanding programs in women’s empowerment, education, and healthcare to build stronger, self-reliant communities.

  • Our Belief

    We believe that every act of care, no matter how small, has the power to change lives. By walking together with communities, we aim to create pathways to dignity, opportunity, and hope.

What We Do

Education & Skills

We support learning and vocational programs that inspire growth, unlock opportunities, and nurture self-reliance in the next generation

Women's Empowerment

We invest in women through skill-building programs and funding women-led small businesses. These initiatives foster financial independence, sustainable livelihoods, and stronger communities.

Healthcare Access

We bring essential medical services and resources to underserved communities, helping families lead healthier and stronger lives

Stories of Impact

Hear from those whose lives have been transformed through our programs

"A widow, Mrs. J Shaikh (name changed for security reasons), who is a mother of three girls, was recently admitted to aadhar Hospital, Dehu Road, for a medical operation. Due to her difficult financial situation, she was unable to pay the full hospital bill after the surgery. Upon learning about her case through one of our dedicated volunteers, Carevia Foundation immediately stepped in to help. The Foundation contributed ₹3,000 to clear the remaining hospital dues, ensuring that the patient could be discharged smoothly and return home. This timely support brought great relief to Mrs. J Shaikh and her family, reflecting Carevia Foundation’s ongoing commitment to assisting those in urgent medical and financial need. "
